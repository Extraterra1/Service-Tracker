## Design Context

### Users
Desk staff use Service Tracker throughout a shift to coordinate daily rental-car deliveries and pickups. They need to scan the operation quickly, update service state with confidence, and reach frequent overview actions—especially Leaderboard and Arquivar Item—with minimal navigation.

### Brand Personality
Practical, calm, and trustworthy. Keep the established compact operational tone: a clear working tool rather than a decorative dashboard.

### Aesthetic Direction
Preserve the existing “Daylight Service Board” direction documented in `DESIGN.md`: dense but legible, restrained neutral surfaces, purposeful red accents, tactile compact controls, and light/dark themes. Avoid flashy SaaS styling, low-density card layouts, visual clutter, and gamified decoration.

### Design Principles

- Put the most frequent shift actions first and keep them one tap away.
- Group choices by the desk staff’s mental model: daily work, lookup/reporting, administration, and account/settings.
- Use progressive disclosure for infrequent or administrative controls.
- Preserve fast scanning through short labels, clear section boundaries, and restrained visual hierarchy.
- Keep destructive, diagnostic, and account actions away from routine operational actions.
