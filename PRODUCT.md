# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Operations staff for a rental-car service workflow. They use the app throughout the workday on phones and other field devices to see upcoming deliveries and pickups, coordinate the daily service list, and mark each delivery or return as done after completing it.

## Product Purpose

Service Tracker keeps the daily rental-car operations list current, shared, and easy to act on. It exists so staff can quickly see what is coming next, confirm completed work, and trust that checklist state stays synchronized across the team without adding extra administrative friction.

## Brand Personality

Calm, utilitarian, easy to read. The product should feel dependable and work-focused, with quiet confidence rather than decorative polish. It should support repeated use during a busy day and make the next action obvious at a glance.

## Anti-references

Avoid clutter that makes the service list harder to scan. Do not push the interface toward flashy SaaS dashboards, decorative marketing styling, oversized visual effects, low-density card sprawl, toy-like gamification, or status treatments that compete with the actual delivery and pickup information.

## Design Principles

1. Prioritize the service list. Every screen should protect the visibility, order, and scan speed of deliveries and pickups.
2. Make completion effortless. Marking a delivery or return as done should feel immediate, obvious, and hard to misread.
3. Design for daylight and motion. Text, contrast, tap targets, and spacing should hold up on phones in bright outdoor conditions and during quick glances between tasks.
4. Keep operations calm. Use restrained hierarchy, stable layouts, and clear states so the app reduces cognitive load instead of adding noise.
5. Preserve team trust. Realtime status, errors, access states, and refresh behavior should communicate clearly enough that staff understand what is current and what needs attention.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target strong contrast, readable type on mobile devices in daylight, generous tap targets, reduced-motion-friendly interactions, and status indicators that do not rely on color alone. Because the app may be glanced at in a vehicle or between field tasks, critical information should be legible quickly and without fine visual discrimination.
